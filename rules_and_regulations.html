Rules and Regulations

World Cross Relay

1. Organization

1.1 World Cross Relay

The sport will be referred to as World Cross Relay, hereforth referred to as WXR.

1.2 International Cross Relay Association

The governing body of the WXR is the International Cross Relay Association, hereforth referred to as the IXRA (pronounced ikra).

2. Events

2.1 Courses

A WXR event must take place on a WXR sanctioned course.

2.1.1 Course Definition

A WXR sanctioned course must be a point to point loop; that is, it must start and end at the same location.

2.1.2 Course Length

Courses must be between 2 and 4 km long, as measured by either a sample of at least 20 separate GPS devices, or a measuring wheel.

2.1.3 Course Elevation Profile

Courses must have at least 25 meters of total elevation gain per loop.

3. Teams

3.1 Team Definition

3.2 Number of Teams

At any given WXR event there shall be no more than 12 teams competing.

3.3 Required Competition

To be eligible for team championship contention, a team must compete in at least 80% of all WXR events and miss no more than 5 WXR events.

3.4 Team Size

To be eligible for competiition, a WXR team must have no more than 15, and no fewer than 5, athletes with paid contracts. No more than 5 additional unpaid athletes may be allowed to practice, travel, or be otherwise affiliated with the team.
